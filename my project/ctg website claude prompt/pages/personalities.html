Famous Personalities from Chittagong

Celebrating the heroes, artists, and achievers who shaped our history

Freedom Fighter

Surya Sen (Masterda)

Revolutionary Leader (1894-1934)

Known as "Masterda," Surya Sen was a Bengali revolutionary who led the famous Chittagong Armoury Raid in 1930. His fearless leadership against British colonial rule inspired millions and made him a legendary figure in India's independence movement.

Major Achievements:

  • Led Chittagong Armoury Raid (April 18, 1930)
  • Established Independent Chittagong (briefly)
  • Inspired revolutionary movements across India
  • National hero of Bangladesh
Politics

A.K. Fazlul Huq

Sher-e-Bangla (1873-1962)

Known as "Sher-e-Bangla" (Tiger of Bengal), A.K. Fazlul Huq was a legendary political leader. Born in Satkhira but had strong connections with Chittagong. He played a crucial role in the Lahore Resolution of 1940 and served as Prime Minister of Bengal.

Major Contributions:

  • Prime Minister of Bengal (1937-1943)
  • Introduced Debt Settlement Act for farmers
  • Founded Krishak Praja Party
  • Key figure in Pakistan Movement
Politics

Major Ziaur Rahman

Former President of Bangladesh (1936-1981)

Born in Bogra but deeply connected to Chittagong, Ziaur Rahman announced Bangladesh's independence from Kalurghat Radio Station in Chittagong. He served as the 6th President of Bangladesh and founded the Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP).

Major Achievements:

  • Declared Independence from Chittagong (1971)
  • Sector Commander in Liberation War
  • President of Bangladesh (1977-1981)
  • Founded BNP political party
Literature

Syed Mujtaba Ali

Renowned Author (1904-1974)

One of the greatest Bengali writers and linguists. Born in Sylhet but spent significant time in Chittagong. Known for his witty writing style and travelogues, particularly "Deshe Bideshe" which remains a classic in Bengali literature.

Literary Works:

  • "Deshe Bideshe" (travel memoir)
  • "Chacha Kahini" (humorous stories)
  • Fluent in 14 languages
  • Awarded Ekushey Padak (1975)
Arts & Music

Sabina Yasmin

Legendary Playback Singer (1953-present)

Born in Chittagong, Sabina Yasmin is one of the most acclaimed playback singers in Bangladesh. With a career spanning over five decades, she has sung over 15,000 songs and won numerous national and international awards.

Achievements:

  • 13 National Film Awards
  • Independence Day Award (1996)
  • Ekushey Padak (1984)
  • Over 15,000 songs recorded
Sports

Akram Khan

Former Cricket Captain (1968-present)

Born in Chittagong, Akram Khan captained the Bangladesh national cricket team and was a key figure in Bangladesh gaining Test status. Known for his leadership and batting prowess.

Cricket Career:

  • Captained Bangladesh in their first Test match
  • Former BCB President
  • Played 8 Tests and 44 ODIs
  • Instrumental in Bangladesh cricket development
Literature

Kazi Nazrul Islam

National Poet (1899-1976)

While not born in Chittagong, Nazrul Islam worked as a journalist at "Nabajug" newspaper in Chittagong in the 1920s. His revolutionary poetry and songs inspired the freedom movement. He is the National Poet of Bangladesh.

Legacy:

  • National Poet of Bangladesh
  • Wrote "Bidrohi" (The Rebel)
  • Composed over 3,000 songs
  • Independence Award (posthumously)
Freedom Fighter

Pritilata Waddedar

Revolutionary (1911-1932)

Born in Chittagong, Pritilata was a Bengali revolutionary nationalist who fought against British colonial rule. She led a group of revolutionaries to attack the Pahartali European Club in 1932, becoming one of India's first women martyrs.

Revolutionary Acts:

  • First Indian woman to lead an armed attack
  • Participated in Chittagong uprising
  • Headmistress despite young age
  • Symbol of women empowerment
Philanthropist

Haji Mohammed Mohsin

Great Philanthropist (1732-1812)

One of the greatest philanthropists in the history of Bengal. Though from Hooghly, he donated vast estates in Chittagong for educational and charitable purposes. The Mohsin Fund continues to support students even today.

Philanthropic Work:

  • Donated entire estate to charity
  • Mohsin Fund supports education
  • Built schools and mosques
  • Helped thousands of poor students

About Chittagong

Chittagong is Bangladesh's second-largest city and the country's main seaport. Known for its natural beauty, it's a perfect destination for tourists seeking both adventure and relaxation.
