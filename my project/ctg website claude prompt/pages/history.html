The Story of Chittagong

Chittagong, known as চট্টগ্রাম in Bengali, is one of the oldest ports in the Bay of Bengal. The city's history spans over a millennium, with references found in ancient texts and chronicles. Its strategic location made it a coveted prize for various kingdoms and colonial powers throughout history.

The name "Chittagong" is believed to have derived from the Arabic word "Shat Ganga" (where the Ganges flows) or from "Chatigaon" (village of Chatis). The city has witnessed the rise and fall of kingdoms, colonial rule, and ultimately became an integral part of modern Bangladesh.

Historical Timeline

4th Century

Ancient Period

Early settlements and trade activities. The region was part of the Samatata and Harikela kingdoms. Chinese travelers' accounts mention this as an important port.

9th-10th Century

Arab Traders

Arab merchants established trade routes. The port became known for exporting fine muslin, silk, and spices to the Middle East and beyond.

1340

Sultanate Rule

Fakhruddin Mubarak Shah annexed Chittagong to the Bengal Sultanate. The city flourished as a major trading hub under Muslim rule.

1538-1666

Arakanese Period

Chittagong was ruled by the Arakanese Kingdom of Mrauk U. Portuguese traders and pirates also operated from here during this period.

1666

Mughal Conquest

Buzurg Umed Khan conquered Chittagong for the Mughal Empire under Aurangzeb. The city was renamed "Islamabad" and became a major port.

1760

British East India Company

The British took control of Chittagong. It became the main port for East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) and a center for tea and jute trade.

1930

Freedom Movement

Chittagong became a stronghold of the independence movement. The famous Chittagong Armoury Raid led by Surya Sen marked a significant moment.

1947

Partition of India

Chittagong became part of East Pakistan. The port continued to grow as the main sea gateway for the eastern region.

1971

Liberation War

Chittagong played a crucial role in Bangladesh's independence. The Declaration of Independence was broadcast from Kalurghat Radio Station.

Present Day

Modern Chittagong

Today, Chittagong is Bangladesh's commercial capital, housing the country's main seaport and contributing significantly to the national economy.

Cultural Heritage

Chittagong's culture is a beautiful blend of Bengali traditions with influences from Arab, Persian, and tribal communities.

🗣️

Chittagonian Dialect

The Chittagonian language (চাটগাঁইয়া or চিটাইঙ্গা) is a distinct Indo-Aryan language spoken by over 13 million people. It has significant phonological and lexical differences from standard Bengali.

🎭

Traditional Arts

Rich tradition of folk music, dance, and theater. Maizbhandari music and Jari-Sari performances are popular cultural expressions unique to the region.

🍲

Cuisine

Famous for Mezbani beef curry (গরুর মাংস), dried fish (শুটকি), and unique seafood preparations. The cuisine is spicier than other regions of Bangladesh.

🎉

Festivals

Celebrates all major Bengali festivals plus unique local celebrations. The Eid-ul-Azha Mezbani tradition is particularly grand in Chittagong.

🏺

Handicrafts

Traditional crafts include bamboo products, pottery, and handloom textiles. Tribal communities contribute unique handicrafts and weaving traditions.

🕌

Religious Harmony

Home to diverse religious communities living in harmony - Muslims, Hindus, Buddhists, and Christians. Many ancient temples, mosques, and churches dot the landscape.

Interesting Historical Facts

⚓ Ancient Port City

Chittagong was mentioned by the great Chinese traveler Fahien in the 5th century and later by Ibn Battuta in the 14th century as a major port in the region.

🎙️ Birthplace of Independence

The Declaration of Independence of Bangladesh was first broadcast from Kalurghat Radio Station in Chittagong on March 26, 1971, making it a sacred site in Bangladesh's history.

⚔️ Chittagong Armoury Raid

On April 18, 1930, revolutionaries led by Surya Sen (Masterda) raided the Chittagong armoury, a bold act against British colonial rule that inspired the freedom movement.

🌍 Strategic Importance

During World War II, Chittagong served as a crucial supply base for Allied forces fighting in the Burma campaign. The Commonwealth War Cemetery commemorates those who died.

🏛️ Cultural Melting Pot

The city has been influenced by Bengali, Arab, Persian, Portuguese, Arakanese, and British cultures, creating a unique multicultural heritage that's reflected in its architecture, language, and traditions.

About Chittagong

Chittagong is Bangladesh's second-largest city and the country's main seaport. Known for its natural beauty, it's a perfect destination for tourists seeking both adventure and relaxation.
