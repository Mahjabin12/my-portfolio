A bit about me

I started designing the way most people do — making things look nice. Over time, I realized the interesting work was upstream: figuring out what to build, for whom, and why the simplest version is usually the best one.

Today I work on interfaces that need to be more useful than loud. I care about visual hierarchy, simple navigation, accessibility, and design decisions that make real user tasks easier.

How I design

  • Slow down to speed up.

    Most design problems are misframed problems. I spend extra time on the question before any pixels.

  • Defaults are design.

    The choice you do not make for the user becomes the choice they live with. I take defaults seriously.

  • Type carries voice.

    Typography is one of the fastest ways to make a product feel intentional, trustworthy, and clear.

  • Ship to learn.

    A prototype in real hands beats a perfect static screen. I design toward the smallest honest version.
